About

I am a Ph.D. candidate at Paul G. Allen School of Computer Science & Engineering, University of Washington, advised by Professor Yejin Choi. I am also a graduate research intern at NVIDIA and was previously a student researcher at Allen Institute for Artificial Intelligence (AI2). I earned B.A. in Computer Science and B.A. in Mathematics from Colby College.

My research centers on humanistic AI safety, aiming to foster the synergistic, secure, and sustainable coexistence of AI and society‚Äîultimately shaping the co-evolution of AI and humanity. My work spans from developing human-centered, continually-evolving AI systems to advancing knowledge about individuals and society through robust innovations in data, learning algorithms, and system design.

My current research encompasses topics such as pluralistic alignment, self-improving algorithms for steerable and secure language models, anticipatory strategies for long-term risks‚Äîincluding overreliance and the erosion of human creativity‚Äîand socially impactful applications, such as clinical AI.
